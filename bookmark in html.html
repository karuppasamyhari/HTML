Cranial Nerves

Inside the cranium, there are 12 nerves, called cranial nerves they are

12.Go to Hypoglossal nerve

1.Olfactory nerve

The first is the olfactory nerve, which allows for your sense of smell.

2.Optic nerve

The optic nerve governs eyesight.

3.Oculomotor nerve

The oculomotor nerve controls pupil response and other motions of the eye, and branches out from the area in the brainstem where the midbrain meets the pons.

4.Trochlear nerve

The trochlear nerve controls muscles in the eye. It emerges from the back of the midbrain part of the brainstem.

5.Trigeminal nerve

The trigeminal nerve is the largest and most complex of the cranial nerves, with both sensory and motor function. It originates from the pons and conveys sensation from the scalp, teeth, jaw, sinuses, parts of the mouth and face to the brain, allows the function of chewing muscles, and much more.

6.Abducens nerve

The abducens nerve innervates some of the muscles in the eye.

7.Facial nerve

The facial nerve supports face movement, taste, glandular and other functions.

8.Vestibulocochlear nerve

The vestibulocochlear nerve facilitates balance and hearing.

9.Glossopharyngeal nerve

The glossopharyngeal nerve allows taste, ear and throat movement, and has many more functions.

10.Vagus nerve

The vagus nerve allows sensation around the ear and the digestive system and controls motor activity in the heart, throat and digestive system.

11.Accessory nerve

The accessory nerve innervates specific muscles in the head, neck and shoulder.

12.Hypoglossal nerve

The hypoglossal nerve supplies motor activity to the tongue.
